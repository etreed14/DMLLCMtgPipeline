**Teradata (TDC) — Facts**
──────────────────────
**Financial:**
- Teradata has been described as a "shit show" for about a decade or more, with some improvement last year.
- The stock moved from $32 to $22, leaving a mark.
- The company has a maintenance business that is considered very good, with recurring revenue.
- The bulk case is that customers find it difficult to get rid of Teradata due to its metastasizing nature.
- The company has a new CFO with a history of selling to private equity.
- Teradata's valuation on maintenance is worth $35-$37.
- Private equity should pay north of $34 for Teradata.
- The company was trimming stock at $50 a year and a half ago.
- The stock is trading at $22 due to an inability to grow the business.

**Edge/Tech:**
- Teradata is involved in fraud alerts, providing real-time, immediate alerts for banks.
- The company handles both structured and unstructured data but primarily focuses on structured data.
- Large language models add value, and structured data does not require models.
- Transition to enterprise AI should benefit Teradata due to its clean data and reliability.

**AI Use Cases:**
- The transition from hyperscalers to enterprise AI is expected to benefit Teradata.
- Consulting activity for AI is picking up, with companies like Accenture, EPAM, and Cognizant involved.
- Enterprise AI adoption is expected to rise, benefiting companies like Snowflake and Teradata.

**Challenges:**
- All growth has been on the unstructured side, and enterprises have not pushed into AI in the same way as hyperscalers.
- The stock is considered a "shit show" and has been a bit of a mess.
- The company has a very bespoke, customized solution that cannot be easily replaced.

**Activism:**
- Lynn Rockway, an activist, owns almost 10% of Teradata and has been increasing her stake.
- The company has a new CFO who has sold to private equity before.

**Market Position:**
- Teradata has a huge install base of Fortune 100 companies.
- Most solutions will need to be hybrid, not just cloud-only.

**Investment Thesis:**
- Teradata is considered one-third pregnant, with fewer obstructions for a deal.
- The company is seen as a low-cost provider with a focus on structured data.
- The stock's valuation is underpinned by its recurring maintenance business.

**Risks:**
- The inability to grow the business has led to its current trading price.
- The stock has been a "shit show" due to its inability to capitalize on growth opportunities.

**Outlook:**
- The company is expected to benefit from the transition to enterprise AI and hybrid solutions.
- The stock is considered a regular, good-for-a-me guy, with potential for growth if the enterprise AI transition materializes.
