STAGE B — Fact Ledger

**TERADATA (TDC) — Facts**

**Financial:**
- Teradata's stock experienced a significant drop from $32 to $22, which left a mark on its trading history.
- The company has a recurring maintenance business that is highly valued, with a maintenance multiple suggesting a valuation of $35-$37.
- Private equity is expected to pay north of $34 for Teradata.

**Edge/Tech:**
- Teradata is known for its real-time fraud alerts, particularly useful for banks needing immediate alerts for suspicious credit card activities.
- The company handles both structured and unstructured data, with a focus on structured data for high-reliability enterprise applications.

**AI Use Cases:**
- The transition from AI hyperscalers to enterprise is expected to benefit Teradata, with a focus on vertical, key-oriented models and more inference.
- Consulting activity for AI is picking up, with companies like Accenture, EPAM, and Cognizant involved, indicating potential growth in enterprise AI adoption.

**Challenges:**
- The company's inability to grow its business has been a concern, with current growth primarily on the unstructured side.
- Enterprises have not fully pushed into AI, with more growth seen on the hyperscaler side.

**Market Position:**
- Teradata has a large install base of Fortune 100 companies, suggesting a strong market position.
- The company is expected to benefit from a rising tide in AI adoption, particularly with hybrid solutions rather than cloud-only solutions.

**Activist Involvement:**
- An activist investor, Lynn Rockway, owns almost 10% of Teradata and has been increasing her stake.
- The company has a new CFO with a history of selling to private equity, indicating potential strategic moves.

**Future Outlook:**
- Teradata is considered to be "one third pregnant," suggesting potential for future deals or strategic changes.
- The company is expected to add to its position in the summer, indicating confidence in its future prospects.
